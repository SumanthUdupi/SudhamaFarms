# Kalki Avatar - Research

## Significance in Hinduism
- **Final Avatar**: Kalki is the prophesied tenth and final avatar of Lord Vishnu.
- **Purpose**: He is foretold to appear at the end of the Kali Yuga, the current age of darkness and moral decline, to restore righteousness (Dharma) and usher in a new era of truth and virtue (Satya Yuga).
- **Depiction**: Kalki is often depicted as a warrior riding a white horse, wielding a sword to vanquish evil.

## Prophecies
- **Appearance**: According to Hindu scriptures, Kalki will be born in the village of Shambhala.
- **Role**: He will destroy the forces of evil, protect the righteous, and re-establish a moral and spiritual order in the world.
- **Symbol of Hope**: Kalki represents a symbol of hope for believers, assuring them that even in the darkest of times, divine intervention will restore balance and righteousness.
