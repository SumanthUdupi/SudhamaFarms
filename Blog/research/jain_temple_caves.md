# Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves - Research

## Historical Background
- **Time Period**: The caves were carved out in the 1st century BCE during the reign of King Kharavela.
- **Purpose**: They were created as residential blocks for Jain monks.
- **Names**: Udayagiri means "Sunrise Hill" and has 18 caves. Khandagiri has 15 caves.

## Sthala Purana & Significance
- **Jainism**: The caves are of great religious importance for Jains, with ornate carvings and inscriptions.
- **Ranigumpha**: The most significant cave in Udayagiri, it is a double-storeyed monastery.
- **Hathigumpha Inscription**: This inscription in Udayagiri is the main source of information about King Kharavela.
- **Cultural Heritage**: The caves reflect the influence of Jainism, Buddhism, and Hinduism, showcasing the artistic and religious tolerance of the era.

## Location Details
- **City**: Bhubaneswar, Odisha.
- **Distance**: Located about 10 km from Bhubaneswar's main railway station and 6 km from the airport.

## Visitor Information
- **Timings**: Open from morning to evening. It's advisable to check the exact timings before visiting.
- **Entry Fee**: There is an entry fee for visitors.
- **Things to Note**:
  - Photography is allowed.
  - It is advisable to wear comfortable footwear.
  - Carry drinking water.
