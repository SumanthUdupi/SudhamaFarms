# Lingaraj Temple - Research

## Historical Background
- **Commissioned**: In the 11th century by King Jajati Keshari of the Somvanshi dynasty.
- **Ancient Worship**: Legends suggest that the Swayambhu (self-manifested) Shivalinga within the temple was being worshipped as early as the 7th century.
- **Significance**: The temple is a symbol of the convergence of Shaivism and Vaishnavism, with the presiding deity being Harihara, a form of both Shiva and Vishnu.

## Sthala Purana & Mythology
- **Main Deity**: The temple is dedicated to a Swayambhu Shivalinga, which is 8 feet in diameter and 8 inches in height.
- **Architectural Style**: The temple is a fine example of Kalinga architecture, with a towering Vimana (180 feet high), Jagamohana (assembly hall), Nata Mandira (festival hall), and Bhoga Mandapa (hall of offering).
- **Temple Complex**: The complex is vast, with over 150 smaller shrines. The walls are adorned with intricate carvings depicting tales from Hindu mythology.

## Location Details
- **City**: Bhubaneswar, Odisha.
- **How to Reach**:
  - **Airport**: Biju Patnaik International Airport (BBI), approximately 7 km away.
  - **Railway**: Bhubaneswar Railway Station (BBS).

## Visitor Information
- **Timings**: 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM.
- **Entry**: Access to the temple is restricted to Hindus. Non-Hindus can view the temple from a designated platform.
- **Best Time to Visit**: October to March.
- **Festivals**:
  - **Mahashivratri**: The most important festival, celebrated with great fervor.
  - **Ashokastami**: The Rath Yatra (car festival) of Lord Lingaraj.
  - **Chandan Yatra**: A 21-day festival where the deities are taken on a ceremonial procession in Bindu Sarovar Lake.
